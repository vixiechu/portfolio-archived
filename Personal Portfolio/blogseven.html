♠ Logo & Banner Creation ♠

I’m the first to admit I’m not the biggest fan of using Adobe Photoshop (version: CC 2015), with that being mentioned, I must admit that I’ve grown to appreciate it a lot more. Granted, my experience of it was very limited until I began my course in 2015; I always thought it was an easy excuse to take lower quality photos, but I do stand entirely corrected. It is an excellent piece of software; especially when enhancing images for the web. There’s lots of useful articles and guidelines available online that can help you to understand why optimisation is important, such as this one.

While using Photoshop, I learnt a lot of things (including the importance of preserving the original image, and working from a copy… again!). For example, I used this font; which I had to download from google and install to Adobe Photoshop. I originally opted to use a free online logo creator, but the image quality was incredibly poor, and it ultimately looked blurry; regardless of my attempts at optimisation. I used the one I created online as a basis; but decided to remove the background entirely as it was originally shrouded in a rectangular shape. I removed this, so that the logo would sit on a background decided by my CSS; meaning it would *hopefully* display well on my website.

I mostly used the following tools to create my logo; transform & free-transform, scale, text input, and paint bucket. I already knew the hex codes of the colours I wanted to include (picked from my website), so I had to manually input them into the colour picker.

Inspiration

My logo was inspired by the 8-bit video games of the 70s & 80s; for which I will always have a special place in my heart. (If you’re interested and have a few spare minutes, here’s a Popular Top 10 8-bit games video to watch!) I chose this style as it reflects one of my dearest passions; video gaming. I’ll never forget when I first played the Atari 3000 with my parents at 3 years old, nor will I forget how difficult some of the games were! I wanted to ensure I included a bit of personality into the banner & logo of my website.

For the banner, I wanted to create a longer image with the intention of it sitting across the top of the page you are currently on; and this gave me a little more room to play with in terms of pixel size. It is 1000 x 250; but designed to optimise depending on the users screen size. You can see this at the top of this page. I created a simpler, square design with a resolution of 200 x 200. Maybe for use as a potential avatar, or maybe even a blog sign off…
